How to Artificially Inflate the Price of a House

6 easy tricks to scam young people looking to start their lives

1. Perform Pointless Renovations That Will Not Last

In the grand tradition of home improvement, why settle for sensible upgrades when you can embark on a journey of utterly frivolous renovations? Picture this: instead of addressing that pesky leaky roof or crumbling foundation, why not install a gold-plated toilet seat or paint over and ruin that beautiful stone fireplace. Transform your living room with an indoor waterfall that, while stunning, will inevitably flood your basement and provide a delightful breeding ground for mosquitoes. And don’t forget the state-of-the-art smart fridge that is so advanced it requires a PhD to operate that can't even dispense ice cubes. Remember these things are to inflate the price tag, they dont have to last more than a year.


2. Create a Bidding frenzy

Get your friends to pretend to be interested buyers. Have them make ridiculously high offers, then casually decline them while whispering about how “there are at least five other people interested.” Nothing says “worth it” like a good old-fashioned bidding war that’s about as real as the chance you will ever be able to buy a home


3. Host a Exclusive Open House

Serve overpriced champagne and artisanal cheese at your open house. Make sure to play classical music and hire a violinist to serenade potential buyers. They’ll be so enchanted by the ambiance that they’ll forget they’re looking at a house with wiring done by a madman overpriced by at least $60,000.


4. Use "Modern" Art Installations:

Place some bizarre art in the living room that no one understands but looks “deep.” Call it modern art and insist that it increases the home’s value by at least 20%. Who can argue with that? Bonus points if literally everything in the house is muted and white. If your house doesn't blind you from light reflecting off of white surfaces you are doing something wrong


5. Be a Contrarian

This is your chance to be creative, just start doing things that defy all logic and common sense for the sake of being "different". Create a sense of urgency by advertising that the house will be available for purchase only during a lunar eclipse. Sprinkle in some deliberately misleading neighborhood gossip. Paint everything strange colours and destroy beautiful historic features of the house and replace them. By flipping conventional wisdom on its head, you’ll have buyers clamoring to pay more than it’s worth, all while scratching their heads in confusion about why they suddenly need to live in a house with “historic potential” (read: cracked foundations).


6. Just Straight Up Lie

Nobody is gonna fact check you, just make up whatever you want. Does this house have asbestos? Do you want it to? Then No.
